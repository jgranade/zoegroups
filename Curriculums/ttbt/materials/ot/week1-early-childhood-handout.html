Through The Bible Together

Week 1: Creation and the Fall

Old Testament Session
Early Childhood (1st and below)

Note for Parents/Caregivers

This handout is designed for you to read with your child. Use simple words, act out the stories, and ask basic questions like "Who made the animals?" or "How do you think Adam felt?" Focus on God's love and care rather than complex theological concepts.

This Week's Focus

Learn about how God made everything beautiful and special, including people! Discover that even when people make bad choices, God still loves them very much.

Memory Verses (Choose One to Focus On)

Day 1: God Makes Everything

Bible Reading for Parents

Genesis 1:1 - 2:3

Tell Me the Story

A long time ago, there was nothing! No trees, no animals, no people. But God was there.

God said, "Let there be light!" and LIGHT appeared! Then God made the sky and the land and the oceans.

God made all the plants - big trees and little flowers. He made the sun to shine in the day and the moon and stars to twinkle at night.

Then God made fish to swim in the water and birds to fly in the sky. He made all the animals - elephants and puppies and bunnies!

And then God made people! He made them very special - different from all the animals. God loved everything He made!

Day 2: The First People

Bible Reading for Parents

Simple retelling of Genesis 2:7-9, 15-25

Tell Me the Story

God made the first man and named him Adam. God made a beautiful garden for Adam to live in. It had yummy fruit to eat and was perfectly safe.

God gave Adam a job - to take care of the garden and all the animals. Adam got to name all the animals! "You're a giraffe! You're a zebra!"

But Adam was lonely. "It's not good for Adam to be alone," God said. So God made a woman named Eve to be Adam's best friend and helper.

Adam and Eve were so happy! They could talk to God anytime they wanted. Everything was perfect!

Day 3: A Bad Choice

Bible Reading for Parents

Simple retelling of Genesis 3:1-13, 20-24

Tell Me the Story

God had one rule in the garden: "Don't eat the fruit from that special tree, or something very sad will happen."

But a sneaky snake came and lied to Eve. "Go ahead and eat it! Nothing bad will happen!" the snake said.

Eve looked at the fruit. It looked so good! She took some and ate it. She gave some to Adam too, and he ate it.

Right away, Adam and Eve felt scared and sad. They had disobeyed God! They tried to hide from God, but you can't hide from God.

God was sad that Adam and Eve disobeyed, but He still loved them. He made clothes for them and promised that one day, everything would be made right again.

Day 4: Two Brothers

Bible Reading for Parents

Simple retelling of Genesis 4:1-12

Tell Me the Story

Adam and Eve had two sons - Cain and Abel. They grew up and had jobs. Cain grew plants and Abel took care of sheep.

Both brothers wanted to give presents to God. Abel gave God his very best sheep. But Cain didn't give his best vegetables to God.

God was happy with Abel's gift, but not with Cain's gift. This made Cain very angry and jealous of his brother.

Cain was so angry that he hurt his brother Abel very badly. God was very sad about what Cain did.

Even though Cain did something terrible, God still protected him. God loves everyone, even when they make very bad choices.

What We Learned

God Made Everything: God made all the beautiful things we see - animals, plants, people!
People Are Special: God made people extra special and loves them very much.
Bad Choices Hurt: When we don't obey God, it makes everyone sad.
God Still Loves Us: Even when we make mistakes, God never stops loving us.
