Week 1: Creation and the Fall

Old Testament Session
Youth (6th-12th)

This Week's Focus

Journey through God's perfect creation, the first humans made in His image, and the devastating effects of sin entering the world. Discover how even in judgment, God shows mercy and promises hope.

Memory Verses

"In the beginning God created the heavens and the earth."

Genesis 1:1

"So God created man in His own image; in the image of God He created him; male and female He created them."

Genesis 1:27
Berean Standard Bible

Day 1: Creation

Today's Reading

Genesis 1:1 - 2:3

Reflections

  • What does it mean that God created everything "good"? How should this affect how we treat creation?
  • How does being made "in God's image" make humans special? What does this mean for how we see ourselves and others?
  • Why did God rest on the seventh day? What can we learn about balance in our lives?
  • How is the creation account different from what the world teaches about origins?

Day 2: Adam and Eve

Today's Reading

Genesis 2:4-24

Reflections

  • What was Adam's job in the Garden of Eden? What does this tell us about God's purpose for work?
  • Why did God say "it is not good for man to be alone"? What does this say about relationships?
  • What does "one flesh" mean in marriage? How does this shape our understanding of dating and relationships?
  • How does the perfect relationship between Adam and Eve challenge us in our friendships and family relationships?

Day 3: The Fall

Today's Reading

Genesis 3

Reflections

  • How did the serpent tempt Eve to disobey God? What similar temptations do we face today?
  • What were the consequences of Adam and Eve's sin? How do we see these effects in our world today?
  • How did God show mercy even in His judgment? What hope do we find in this story?
  • Why do you think Adam and Eve tried to hide from God? When do we try to hide from God?

Day 4: Cain and Abel

Today's Reading

Genesis 4

Reflections

  • Why did God accept Abel's offering but not Cain's? What does this teach us about our heart attitude?
  • How did sin spread from Adam and Eve to their children? How do we see sin affecting families today?
  • What does it mean to be your "brother's keeper"? How should we look out for our siblings and friends?
  • How does jealousy and anger (like Cain's) affect relationships in our lives?

This Week's Big Ideas

Creation: God made everything perfect and declared it "very good."
Image of God: Humans are uniquely created to reflect God's character.
The Fall: Sin broke our relationship with God and brought death into the world.
Hope: Even in judgment, God shows mercy and promises a way back to Him.
