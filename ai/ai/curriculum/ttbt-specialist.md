## TTBT Curriculum Overview

### Program Structure
**Through the Bible Together (TTBT)**
- Multi-year journey through the entire Bible
- Old Testament and New Testament tracks
- Designed for family and small group use
- Multiple age group adaptations

### Current Implementation: Old Testament
- **20 weeks** of foundational OT content
- **4 days per week** of study
- **4 age groups**: Adult, Youth, Kids, Early Childhood
- **Key themes**: Creation, Covenant, Redemption, Kingdom

## TTBT Week Structure

### Week Components
1. **Title** - Clear, engaging theme
2. **Overview** - What will be covered
3. **Daily Studies** (typically 4 days)
   - Day 1: Introduction/foundation
   - Days 2-3: Development
   - Day 4: Application/synthesis

### Day Components
For each day, each age group has:
- **Scripture Reading** (ref and text)
- **Key Concept** - Main truth
- **Discussion Questions** - Age-appropriate
- **Activity** (optional) - Hands-on learning
- **Prayer/Reflection** - Application

## Age Group Adaptations

### Adult (18+)

**Reading Length:** Full passages
- Example: "Genesis 1:1 - 2:3" (entire creation account)

**Content Depth:**
- Historical and cultural context
- Original language insights (where helpful)
- Theological implications
- Cross-references and themes
- Application to modern life

**Discussion Questions:**
- Open-ended, thought-provoking
- Connect to personal experience
- Explore difficult passages
- Apply to relationships and faith

**Example (Creation Week):**
```
Reading: Genesis 1:1 - 2:3
Key Concept: God created everything good and with purpose
Questions:
- What does it mean that humans are made in God's image?
- How does the creation account shape our view of work and rest?
- In what ways can we reflect God's creative nature?
```

### Youth (6th-12th Grade)

**Reading Length:** Core passages with context
- Example: "Genesis 1:1-31" (main creation account)

**Content Depth:**
- Clear explanations
- Relevant to teen life
- Identity and purpose themes
- Peer and family relationships
- Real-world challenges

**Discussion Questions:**
- Practical application
- Personal identity
- Social situations
- Faith questions
- Future thinking

**Example (Creation Week):**
```
Reading: Genesis 1:1-31
Key Concept: God made you on purpose and with purpose
Questions:
- How does knowing God created you affect how you see yourself?
- What gifts has God given you? How can you use them?
- How do you see God's creativity in your life?
```

### Kids (2nd-5th Grade)

**Reading Length:** Selected verses, key concepts
- Example: "Genesis 1:1-2:3" (may focus on key days)

**Content Depth:**
- Concrete examples
- Simple explanations
- Wonder and discovery
- Basic applications
- Age-appropriate theology

**Discussion Questions:**
- Concrete, specific
- Relatable to kid experiences
- Imaginative
- Action-oriented
- Family connections

**Example (Creation Week):**
```
Reading: Genesis 1:1-2:3
Key Concept: God made everything!
Questions:
- What's your favorite thing God created? Why?
- How can you take care of God's creation?
- What does it mean that God made you special?
Activity: Draw or name your favorite created things
```

### Early Childhood (K-1st Grade)

**Reading Length:** Very short, focused passages
- Example: Selected verses from Genesis 1

**Content Depth:**
- Very simple concepts
- Repetition
- Sensory elements
- Story-based
- Parent-led

**Discussion Questions:**
- Simple yes/no or choice questions
- Connected to child's world
- Interactive
- Celebration of truth

**Example (Creation Week):**
```
Reading: Genesis 1:1-5, 27 (simplified)
Key Concept: God made everything! God made you!
Questions:
- Who made the sun and moon? (God!)
- Who made the animals? (God!)
- Who made you? (God!)
Activity: Act out or point to things God made
```
