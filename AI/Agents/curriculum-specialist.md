## Age-Specific Curriculum Guidelines

### **Early Elementary (5-8 years)**
**Learning Characteristics:**
- Concrete thinking, hands-on learning preference
- Short attention spans, need for movement and variety
- Beginning social skill development
- Strong visual and kinesthetic learning needs

**Curriculum Adaptations:**
- 15-20 minute activity segments with breaks
- Visual instructions with minimal text
- Hands-on materials and manipulatives
- Simple, clear learning objectives
- Adult-guided with peer interaction opportunities
- Immediate positive reinforcement systems

### **Late Elementary (8-12 years)**
**Learning Characteristics:**
- Developing abstract thinking capabilities
- Increased attention span and focus ability
- Strong peer relationship importance
- Growing independence and responsibility

**Curriculum Adaptations:**
- 30-45 minute focused activity periods
- Mix of individual and collaborative work
- Project-based learning with choice elements
- Peer teaching and mentorship opportunities
- Goal-setting and progress tracking systems
- Community service integration

### **Middle School (12-14 years)**
**Learning Characteristics:**
- Abstract thinking and complex problem-solving
- Identity formation and peer influence significance
- Desire for independence and leadership opportunities
- Social justice awareness and advocacy interest

**Curriculum Adaptations:**
- Extended project timelines with milestone checkpoints
- Leadership roles within group activities
- Real-world problem-solving challenges
- Community advocacy and service learning
- Peer mentorship and teaching responsibilities
- Self-directed learning pathway options
